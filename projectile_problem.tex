\section{Problem Statement}
A projectile is launched from ground level with initial speed $v_0$ at an angle $\theta$ above the horizontal. A vertical wall is located at a horizontal distance $D$ from the launch point.

\bigskip

\textbf{Find:} The launch angle $\theta$ that maximizes the height at which the projectile hits the wall, and the maximum height.

\bigskip

\hrule
\bigskip

\section{Solution}

The equations of motion for projectile motion are:
\begin{align}
x(t) &= v_0 \cos\theta \cdot t \tag{1} \\
y(t) &= v_0 \sin\theta \cdot t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2 \tag{2}
\end{align}

\bigskip

When the projectile hits the wall at $x = D$, the time of flight is:
\begin{align}
D &= v_0 \cos\theta \cdot t \\
t &= \frac{D}{v_0 \cos\theta} \tag{3}
\end{align}

\bigskip

Substitute equation (3) into equation (2) to find the height $y$ as a function of $\theta$:
\begin{align}
y(\theta) &= v_0 \sin\theta \left(\frac{D}{v_0 \cos\theta}\right) - \frac{1}{2}g\left(\frac{D}{v_0 \cos\theta}\right)^2 \\
&= D \tan\theta - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2 \cos^2\theta} \tag{4}
\end{align}

\bigskip

Using the trigonometric identity $\frac{1}{\cos^2\theta} = 1 + \tan^2\theta$:
\begin{align}
y(\theta) &= D \tan\theta - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2}(1 + \tan^2\theta) \tag{5}
\end{align}

\bigskip

Let $u = \tan\theta$. Then:
\begin{align}
y(u) &= D u - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2}(1 + u^2) \\
&= -\frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2}u^2 + D u - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2} \tag{6}
\end{align}

\bigskip

This is a downward-opening parabola. The maximum occurs at the vertex:
\begin{align}
\frac{dy}{du} &= -\frac{g D^2}{v_0^2}u + D = 0 \\
\frac{g D^2}{v_0^2}u &= D \\
u &= \frac{v_0^2}{g D} \tag{7}
\end{align}

\bigskip

Therefore, the optimal angle is:
\begin{align}
\tan\theta &= \frac{v_0^2}{g D} \\
\theta &= \arctan\left(\frac{v_0^2}{g D}\right) \tag{8}
\end{align}

\bigskip

Substitute back to find the maximum height:
\begin{align}
y_{\max} &= D\left(\frac{v_0^2}{g D}\right) - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2}\left[1 + \left(\frac{v_0^2}{g D}\right)^2\right] \\
&= \frac{v_0^2}{g} - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2} - \frac{v_0^2}{2 g} \\
&= \frac{v_0^2}{2 g} - \frac{g D^2}{2 v_0^2} \\
&= \frac{v_0^4 - g^2 D^2}{2 g v_0^2} \tag{9}
\end{align}

\bigskip

\hrule
\bigskip

\section{Final Results}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Optimal launch angle:
    \[
    \boxed{\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{v_0^2}{g D}\right)}
    \]
    
    \item Maximum height at wall:
    \[
    \boxed{y_{\max} = \frac{v_0^4 - g^2 D^2}{2 g v_0^2}}
    \]
\end{itemize}

\bigskip

\textbf{Note:} This solution is valid for $\frac{v_0^2}{g D} > 1$, which ensures the projectile reaches the wall before reaching its maximum range. If $\frac{v_0^2}{g D} \leq 1$, the projectile cannot reach the wall.
